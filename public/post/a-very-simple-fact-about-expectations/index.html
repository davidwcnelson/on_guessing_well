A Very Simple Fact About Expectations

David W.C. Telson · 2020/07/04 · 1 minute read

This one is a short one (just so I can keep up the momentum of posting). I absolutely love the concept of mathematical expectations, and in this very short post I am hoping to show a simple fact that I absolutely love. Specifically, I want to show the relationships between expectations and probabilities.

First things first, let’s remind ourselves of what a probability is. Over simplifying (my apologies to the measure theorists out there), we say that \(P(E)\) is the probability that an event \(E\) occurs if \(P\) satisfies the following axioms:

  1. \(P(E) \in \mathbb{R_{\ge0}}\) i.e. the probability is a non-negative real number.

  2. \(P(\Omega) = 1\) where \(\Omega\) is the set of all possible outcomes.
